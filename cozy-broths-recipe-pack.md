## 1) Golden Chicken Bone Broth Soup

**Best for:** cozy reset days, feeling run down, comfort meals

**Ingredients:**
- 6 cups chicken bone broth
- 1 to 2 cups shredded chicken (rotisserie works great)
- 2 carrots, sliced
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 1/2 onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- Salt to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Bring bone broth to a gentle simmer.
2. Add carrots, celery, onion, and garlic.
3. Simmer 15 to 20 minutes until vegetables are soft.
4. Stir in turmeric and shredded chicken.
5. Let simmer another 5 to 10 minutes.
6. Salt to taste and serve warm.

**Notes:**
- Add rice or noodles if you want it more filling.
- Many people enjoy this when they want something warm and grounding.

## 2) Garlic Onion Comfort Broth

**Best for:** simple comfort, light meals, low appetite days

**Ingredients:**
- 4 cups broth (chicken or veggie)
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1/2 onion, sliced
- 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Heat oil in a pot and lightly sauté garlic and onion until soft.
2. Add broth and bring to a simmer.
3. Let cook 10 to 15 minutes.
4. Season and serve warm.

**Notes:**
- You can strain it or leave it chunky.
- Add toast or crackers on the side for extra comfort.

## 3) Lazy Rotisserie Chicken Soup

**Best for:** busy days, quick comfort meals

**Ingredients:**
- 6 cups broth
- 1 rotisserie chicken, shredded
- 1 cup frozen veggies or chopped carrots and celery
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: noodles or rice

**Instructions:**
1. Add broth and veggies to a pot and bring to a simmer.
2. Cook 10 to 15 minutes.
3. Add shredded chicken.
4. Add noodles or rice if using and cook until done.
5. Season and serve.

**Notes:**
- This is a great low-effort comfort recipe for tired days.

---

## 4) Turmeric Ginger Chicken Soup

**Best for:** cozy evenings, warming meals

**Ingredients:**
- 6 cups broth
- 1 cup cooked chicken
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 carrot, sliced
- Salt to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Bring broth to a simmer.
2. Add ginger, garlic, carrot, and turmeric.
3. Simmer 15 minutes.
4. Add chicken and cook 5 more minutes.
5. Season and serve.

**Notes:**
- Warm, cozy, and slightly bold in flavor.

## 5) Egg Drop Wellness Broth

**Best for:** light meals, quick comfort

**Ingredients:**
- 4 cups broth
- 2 eggs, whisked
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: green onion

**Instructions:**
1. Bring broth to a gentle simmer.
2. Slowly drizzle in whisked eggs while stirring.
3. Let eggs cook into soft ribbons.
4. Season and serve immediately.

**Notes:**
- This takes less than 10 minutes from start to finish.
- Great when you want something warm but very light.

---

## 6) Freezer Scrap Veggie Broth

**Best for:** reducing waste, pantry cooking

**Ingredients:**
- Veggie scraps (carrot peels, onion ends, celery tops, etc.)
- 6 to 8 cups water
- Salt to taste
- Optional: garlic, herbs

**Instructions:**
1. Add everything to a pot.
2. Bring to a boil, then simmer 30 to 60 minutes.
3. Strain out solids.
4. Salt to taste.

**Notes:**
- Store in the fridge or freeze for later.
- Every batch will taste a little different.

---

## 7) Simple Salt and Herb Broth

**Best for:** calm, simple, reset days

**Ingredients:**
- 4 cups broth
- Fresh herbs (parsley, thyme, or rosemary)
- Pinch of salt

**Instructions:**
1. Heat broth gently.
2. Add herbs and let steep 5 to 10 minutes.
3. Remove herbs if desired and serve warm.

**Notes:**
- Minimal, calming, and very easy.
